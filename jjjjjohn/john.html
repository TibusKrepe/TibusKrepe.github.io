John Karel

John Karel is a multidisciplinary artist who uses everyday objects and scenarios to create surreal but relatable scenes and compositions. The backgrounds are often undefined or a repeating pattern that could go on forever.

Karel also works on 2d digital pages, these are rendered with flat shading and a regular line thickness through-out. In these comics, objects are copied and pasted as well as being reused between them. It’s fun that the strips all show time progressing despite taking place in undefined spaces. The pieces play on our understanding of reality.

This piece uses rotoscoping, the scene has a strange quality because the forms are jittery in themselves but they move through the space in a very smooth and steady manner. I like the way they pass through each other. I also appreciate that the scribbled side of the lens accurately describes it’s texture.
